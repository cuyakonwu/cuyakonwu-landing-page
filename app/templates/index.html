Welcome To My World

Hi, my name is Conrad Uyakonwu. I am a Questbridge Scholar pursuing a B.A. and M.S. in Computer Science at the University of Chicago. Drawing from my favorite work of fiction, I view life through the lens of the "salt mountain"—where every experience, whether engineering scalable AI solutions or battling back from injury on the powerlifting platform, is accumulated to build character, resilience, and strength.

About Me

My story starts in Nigeria, where I spent my childhood racing bikes and playing soccer. Shortly before I turned ten, my family immigrated to the United States. Growing up in an underserved community taught me early on that adversity must be met with adaptation. From finding out our only car was repossessed to dedicating over 300 hours to community service, I learned that resilience is built drop by drop. This mindset fueled my journey to The University of Chicago as an Odyssey Scholar.

Whether I am diagnosing Linux kernel bottlenecks as a Meta Fellow, building generative AI pipelines at BILL, or designing AI curriculums for teachers in Burundi through Code Your Dreams, I carry the "salt mountain" philosophy. This idea—that we are formed by the slow accumulation of both our successes and our struggles—is deeply rooted in my passion for powerlifting. Recently, returning from injury, I trusted my training to hit a 722.5kg total at the 5W Midwest Showdown.

The iron hasn't just built personal strength; it's built community. As Co-Founder and President of the UChicago Barbell Club, I've helped grow our team to over 50 members, leading fundraising and spearheading initiatives to pay entry fees for first-time competitors. Life, like the platform, is an accumulation of every heavy step, and I'm dedicated to leveraging my strengths in technology and sports to elevate the voices and opportunities of others around me.
